Defining Star Wars "Creatures"

From the handful of creatures in the original films, the roster of Star Wars creatures has exploded as the franchise has expanded over the last 40+ years to two other trilogies, a growing number of original series and dozens of novelizations.

But what does the term "creature" actually mean in the context of an imaginary galaxy containing thousands of different species and planets? Is there a distinction between creatures and other types of beings in the SW universe?

Aliens vs Creatures

Aliens

According to Wookiepedia," 'Alien' was a general-purpose term used in the galaxy by humans and even some droids to refer to anyone of a sentient species that was not a human."

To name just a few examples from the films, the category of alien included Wookies, Ithorians, Ewoks, Hutts and Twi'leks.

Since there were more than 20 million sentient species in the galaxy, aliens encompassed a vast number of them!

Perhaps a good metaphor is the famous Mos Eisley cantina scene from Episode IV: A New Hope, in which an inexperienced Luke Skywalker was bewildered by the sheer variety of unfamilar beings in that small, dusty bar.

Creatures

The key distinction between aliens and creatures is sentience: self-awareness, abstract thinking, and higher reasoning.

Creatures are generally considered non-sentient, while aliens are sentient. A typical creature is the humble tauntaun, the furry mammal-like lizards ridden by Luke Skywalker & Han Solo on the ice planet Hoth in The Empire Strikes Back.

However, some creatures displayed behaviors that were harder to categorize, and were thus labeled semi-sentient. As seen in the animated series Star Wars Rebels, the giant, whale-like Purrgils can fly through hyperspace without the aid of a ship or any other technology!

Another major difference is that most creatures could not use the Force, while many aliens were Force-sensitive. But some creatures did not fit this pattern clearly. Ysalamiri from the forest planet Myrkr had a unique ability to create a Force-free bubble around their bodies as a defense against Force-sensitive predators.
